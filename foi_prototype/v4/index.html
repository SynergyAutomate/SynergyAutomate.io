Freedom of Information Requests

Before you send a request

Please note that FOI cannot provide personal advice, explanations in relation to departmental matters or progress reports on applications that are in process.

Documents and information available outside the FOI Act

You do not need to make an FOI request if you only want copies of your own evidence or certificates as there are alternative (and quicker) ways to access this information.

  • Your personal visa and citizenship information and details are available on ImmiAccount immediately
  • Visa Entitlement Verification Online system (VEVO) allows visa holders, employers, education providers and other organisations to check visa conditions

The Department may hold information and documentation that has been provided by another authority or is publicly available elsewhere. Please use the correct channels that can provide what you are looking for quicker and easier to access your information.

  • Members of the public requesting information about themselves that is owned by another Government department or agency, please visit the OIAC website.
  • Members of the public requesting copies of personal documents generated by another issuing authority or agency (such as birth/marriage certificates or police clearances), please contact the original issuing authority or agency (could be in your specific state) to obtain an official copy of the source document.
  • Members of the public requesting an update to a report they provided to another Australian authority or agency (such as an update to a police report), please contact the original issuing authority or agency (in your specific state) to update on the report.
  • Previous clients of the Department requesting personal documentation provided by a 3rd party, please contact the 3rd party provider directly (eg IHMS for your health care records).
  • Government departments (Federal, State or Territory) requesting copies of documents about clients of the Department of Home Affairs, please lodge a privacy request and quote the state legislation provision.
  • Media requests such as statistics or information that is not a physical document already published on our website, please contact the Parliamentary liaison Unit (PLU) to release the information under Privacy Act.
  • Departmental staff requests for copies of documentation, please contact the relevant departmental section.

When you submit your request

The ‘scope’ of the request is ensuring you are as specific as possible when describing the documents or information to help us quickly locate what you want to access, as it informs what system or area we need to go to for an answer. Specific files and documents are more likely to be in an electronic format and easier to access quickly, if the document type and reference numbers are provided.

To assist the FOI Section in processing FOI requests quickly, we ask that you please consider how you word your request:

  • provide a scope (refer to our examples) – we often receive requests that have no scope at all
  • provide specific immigration history and which particular file/document is relevant in the first instance
  • ensure that you provide specific details of the files that you are seeking to access – file numbers, date ranges, full names and correct date of birth.

The more document information you share the better:

  • Document reference numbers
  • [see list of ‘scope’ information’]

Avoid using non-specific language or broad terminology such as:

  • “all visa files”
  • “all documents relating to the applicant”
  • “including but not limited to..”
  • “other such documents”
  • “and other relevant files”
  • “Anything that appears on the system under my name and DOB”

Sending a vague request with missing ‘scope’ may add a substantial delay to the finalisation of your FOI request. This is because the information may include paper files which may be stored across the country, and given the COVID-19 pandemic, currently accessing hard copies of files can be difficult.

After you submit your request

Your request will be referred to the appropriate area for assessment.

For more information about how we use your request, please see our Privacy Statement.

Processing Times

The Department will acknowledge receipt of an FOI access request within 14 calendar days of lodgement.

The Department is required to give the client a decision about the request and the reasons for that decision within 30 days of lodgement of the request.

If the Department needs to consult with anyone else, we have a further 30 days to inform the client of the decision – for example, if the client asks for a document which contains information about a third party.

If for any other reason the Department requires more than 30 days to complete the request, we will contact the client to arrange an extension of time up to 30 calendar days for processing the request.

The Department might also apply to the Australian Information Commissioner for an extension of the processing time where exceptional circumstances would prevent us from processing the request in 30 days. The client will be informed in writing if the processing time has been extended.

Do you want to access documents or details provided to the Department?

  1. ImmiAccount gives clients immediate access to e-lodged application forms, supporting documentation and departmental correspondence, such as grant letters.
    • If you'd like a copy of your visa or citizenship application for your own records or your original application was made through ImmiAccount, please login to your ImmiAccount
    • If you’d like to access your Document for Travel to Australia (DFTTA), apply for an ImmiCard.
    • If you'd like a copy of your permanent residency visa or current visa status for your own records and are a long term resident who arrived in Australia before 1990 and have not left the country since your arrival, apply for an ImmiCard.
  2. If you'd like to request to cancel a Temporary Resident visa for you or your partner, please complete form 1194 Certification of Immigration Status
  3. If you’d like to request access to your personal records in order to prepare your application for a subsequent Temporary Protection visa (TPV) or Safe Haven Enterprise visa (SHEV), please complete the Admin Release form.
  4. If you’d like a copy of your visa or citizenship documentation to share with a new migration agent, please contact your previous migration agent as Migration Agents must retain documents/records for 7 years and provide a copy of the documentation to you within 7 days (refer to the Migration Agents Requirements Act).

Do you want to access documents or details owned by the Department?

  1. If you’d like to request evidence of your current visa or citizenship status as proof of permanent residency, permission or visa status, or to apply for work or Medicare or to update details for a new request, visit Visa Entitlement Verification Online (VEVO).
  2. If you'd like to request a copy of your Australian Citizen Certificate for your own records, please complete form 119 Application for evidence of Australian citizenship.
  3. If you'd like to request a visa grant number for your own records, you will need call the DATACOM staff and provide proof of your identity .
  4. If you'd like to request evidence of a deceased person’s citizenship status for your own records, please complete form 1391 Request for confirmation of Australian citizenship status of a deceased person.
  5. If you'd like to request a copy of your International Movement information for your own records, please complete form 1359 Request for international movement records.
  6. If you'd like to request a copy of your Certificate of Immigration Status as proof of Superannuation Status, please complete form 1194 Certification of Immigration Status.

Do you want to update documents or details owned by the Department?

  1. If you’d like to request an update be made to your contact details (address, passport name, contact details or gender or you wish to provide updated identity (marriage, birth certificate) or business documents to the Department, please refer to the Changes in Situation page of the website.
  2. If you think the information the Department owns about you is
    • is incomplete, incorrect, out of date or misleading
    • has been, is being, or is available to be used for an administrative purpose,
    Please complete form 424c Request for amendment or annotation to personal records

If you’d like to access to documents about the Department or the Minister’s office, such as briefs, process documents, statistics relating to visa programs, and emails between areas. This may also include requests in relation to the Department’s policies, procedures, the import/export of goods, the Tourist Refund Scheme (TRS) and the Tariff Classification and Concession scheme.
